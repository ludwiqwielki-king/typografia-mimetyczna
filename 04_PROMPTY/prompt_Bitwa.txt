Rough maritime battle woodcut, heavy distressed black lines on deep navy/black base (90%). Central image: Two frigates emerging from thick fog, locked in close combat (boarding scene). Cannon smoke swirling around ships forms verses of the poem as ghostly text clouds. Title 'Bitwa - Lech Klupś' carved into a broken mast fragment anchor. Key elements: waves below ships tinted crimson/red suggesting 'blood streams' without gore (stylized as red ink in water), wood splinters from damaged hulls carrying micro-text, ghostly faces of comrades faintly visible in the fog/smoke. One pale accent: rust-red for blood/water, pale grey for fog. Mood: dramatic tension, naval brotherhood, violent memory, dreamlike nostalgia. Text is PART OF structure - smoke spells verses, waves carry refrain 'WYGRA TEN KTO UTRZYMA SHIP', wood splinters hold micro-text. Max 30% coverage. Aesthetic: 18th-century naval engraving meets expressionist shanty folk art. CRITICAL: NO graphic gore. Blood is stylized as red water/ink. Focus on the ships, the fog, and the memory of battle, not individual suffering. The scene should feel like a dream remembered by an old sailor. Text should look like it was carved into the smoke and wood during the fight.
