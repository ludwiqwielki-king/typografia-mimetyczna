# Persja – Rumi: "Out Beyond Ideas"

## Tekst
```
"Out beyond ideas of wrongdoing and rightdoing,
there is a field. I'll meet you there.
When the soul lies down in that grass,
the world is too full to talk about.
Ideas, language, even the phrase 'each other'
doesn't make any sense."

Tłumaczenie na polski (interpretacyjne):

"Poza ideami złego czynu i dobrego czynu
jest pole. Spotkam cię tam.
Gdy dusza spocznie na tej trawie,
świat jest zbyt pełny, by o nim mówić.
Ideje, język, nawet fraza 'nawzajem'
nie mają żadnego sensu."

```

## Prompt
```

Expressionist woodcut engraving, heavy continuous black lines, 90% deep black base. Central image: a vast open field under starry night sky, where two silhouettes merge into one as they approach each other. The grass beneath them transforms into flowing calligraphic text of Rumi's poem. Title 'Out Beyond Ideas - Rumi' carved into a stone at field's edge anchor. Key symbols: swirling patterns suggesting whirling dervishes in sky, a tent made of light (not fabric) where boundaries dissolve, stars forming words in multiple languages that blend together. One pale accent: faint gold for starlight, soft silver for grass blades. Mood: mystical unity, transcendent love, wordless communion, spiritual intoxication. Text is PART OF structure - woven into grass, swirling in sky, etched in light. Max 30% coverage. Vintage bookplate aesthetic, high contrast. CRITICAL: Emphasize dissolution of boundaries - figures should blur together, text should flow without clear separation, world should feel boundless. No censorship: embrace Sufi mysticism directly - unity beyond duality, love beyond language.

```
