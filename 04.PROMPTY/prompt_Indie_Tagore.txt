Indian miniature inspired woodcut engraving, heavy black ink lines on deep black base (85%). Central image: Divine hands holding a simple bamboo flute (Bansuri), but the hands are translucent, merging with the landscape behind. Music notes flowing from flute transform into rolling hills, valleys, and river paths carrying poem text as flowing melody lines. Title 'Gitanjali Pieśń 1 - Rabindranath Tagore' carved into the flute itself anchor. Key elements: wood grain of flute spelling micro-verses, breath visible as golden mist forming Sanskrit-like patterns, endless cycle of hills suggesting 'eternally new melodies'. One pale accent: warm gold for breath/music, soft saffron for landscape hints. Mood: spiritual intimacy, devotional surrender, cosmic joy, divine play (Lila). Text is PART OF structure - melody lines are words, wood grain is verse, breath is mist. Max 25% coverage. Aesthetic: Bengal School of Art meets expressionist woodcut. CRITICAL: Avoid clichés (no peacocks, no elephants, no generic yoga). Focus on the flute as soul, hands as divine, music as landscape. The player and instrument should blur into one.
