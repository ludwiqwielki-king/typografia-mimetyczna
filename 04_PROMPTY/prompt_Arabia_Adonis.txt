Contemporary expressionist woodcut engraving, heavy fractured black lines on deep black base (90%). Central image: abstract ruin - not a building, but a conceptual collapse where stone, text, and shadow merge. Fragments of Arabic calligraphy break apart and reform as cracks in stone, wounds in paper, shadows in void. Title 'Początek i Koniec - Adonis' carved into a fractured stone anchor, letters partially eroded. Key elements: desert as negative space (not sand dunes - emptiness as presence), wound-like fissures carrying poem text as bleeding ink, exile figure as silhouette dissolving into landscape. One pale accent: faint rust-red for 'wound/ink', cold grey for 'absence'. Mood: existential fragmentation, creative destruction, exile as home, memory as ruin. Text is PART OF structure - cracks spell verses, erosion reveals words, shadow holds micro-text. Max 30% coverage. Aesthetic: modern Arabic avant-garde meets expressionist woodcut. CRITICAL: NO Orientalist clichés (no camels, no minarets, no geometric patterns for decoration). Focus on abstraction, fragmentation, conceptual ruin. The desert is emptiness that contains, not landscape that decorates. The wound is where text is born, not injury to illustrate.
