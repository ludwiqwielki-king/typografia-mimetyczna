Chinese Shan Shui inspired woodcut engraving, heavy black ink lines on deep black base (85%). Vast cosmic landscape: Yellow River flowing through misty mountains in traditional three-distances perspective (high, deep, level). Single solitary figure stands on rocky outcrop gazing at full moon - not sad but in cosmic communion. Moon's reflection on river surface forms concentric ripples carrying poem text as flowing calligraphy. Title 'Samotność nad Żółtą Rzeką - Li Bai' carved into foreground stone anchor. Key elements: layered mountain ranges fading into mist (qi energy flow), pine trees clinging to cliffs, wine cup beside figure, flowing river as time's passage. One pale accent: faint gold for moonlight, soft ink-wash grey for mist. Mood: cosmic solitude, taoist harmony, expansive loneliness, spiritual intoxication. Text is PART OF structure - river ripples spell verses, mist holds micro-text, stone bears anchor. Max 25% coverage (Liu Bai - leaving white space). Aesthetic: Tang Dynasty landscape painting meets expressionist woodcut. CRITICAL: Emphasize vastness over detail - figure should be small against cosmic scale. No Western perspective - use Chinese scrolling landscape composition. Wine cup present but not emphasized - it's a tool of transcendence, not decoration.
