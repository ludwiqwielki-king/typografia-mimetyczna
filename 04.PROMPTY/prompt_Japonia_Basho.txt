Sumi-e inspired woodcut engraving, heavy black ink lines on deep black base (90%). Extreme minimalism: vast negative space representing stillness. Central focus: a single, small frog mid-jump above an old, mossy pond - captured at the exact moment before impact. Water surface shows concentric ripples forming the three lines of the haiku text, carved as if by the sound itself. Title 'Furuike ya - Matsuo Bashō' etched into a weathered stone at pond's edge anchor. Key elements: moss texture on stones carrying micro-text, reed shadows suggesting season (spring), single drop of water suspended in air. One pale accent: faint ink-wash grey for water, soft green for moss. Mood: profound stillness, sudden interruption, resonant silence, acceptance of transience. Text is PART OF structure - ripples spell the poem, moss holds micro-verses, stone bears the anchor. Max 20% coverage (haiku demands emptiness). Aesthetic: Japanese ink painting meets expressionist woodcut. CRITICAL: Embrace MA (negative space) - what is NOT shown is as important as what is. No cherry blossoms, no moon, no decorative elements. Only: old pond, frog, sound, silence. The jump is a point, not an action. The sound is texture, not illustration.
